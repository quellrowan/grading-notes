## Overall
This is a strong submission. Your reviews are thoughtful, professional, and clearly aligned with the stage of the proposals you are reviewing. You do a good job summarizing the projects accurately, identifying their strongest ideas, and then shifting into feedback that is genuinely developmental rather than merely evaluative. Both reviews feel like they were written by someone trying to help the author improve the next version, which is exactly what this assignment is supposed to do.

The strongest feature of this submission is that your comments are well matched to the stage of the proposals. You are not treating these like final papers. Instead, you focus on narrowing, defining scope, clarifying the analytical center, and strengthening the next step. That is the right approach here.

## What is working well

### 1. The summaries are clear and accurate
In both reviews, you explain the proposal’s main idea in a way that is easy to follow and clearly connected to the actual draft. You do not distort the topic or replace the student’s project with your own interpretation of what it “should” be. That matters. Strong peer review starts with showing that you understood the draft as written.

### 2. Your tone is constructive and professional
You maintain a very good tone throughout. The comments are candid without being harsh, and you consistently frame your feedback as something that can help the author strengthen the next draft. This is particularly important in peer review assignments, where it can be easy either to become too vague and nice or too blunt and critical. You avoid both of those traps.

### 3. Your feedback is genuinely stage-appropriate
This is probably the biggest strength of the submission. For both projects, you focus on the questions that matter most right now:
- Is the scope manageable?
- Is the project centered on one clear line of inquiry?
- Are the concepts grounded enough?
- Is the RE lens still visible?

That is exactly what a developing/mid-stage review should emphasize.

### 4. Your suggestions are actionable
The suggestions go beyond “be more clear” or “narrow the scope.” You actually point toward what narrowing could look like and what should become more explicit in the next version. That makes the review much more useful to the author.

## What to strengthen

### 1. The reviews could be a little more specific in places
Even though the reviews are strong overall, there are a few places where the feedback could become even more useful by being slightly more concrete. For example, when you say a topic should be narrowed or grounded, you could sometimes go one step further and point to one or two specific places in the proposal where the broadness is most visible. The current comments are helpful, but a bit more precision would make them even stronger.

### 2. A few comments could push more directly on the likely next paper structure
You clearly identify conceptual issues, but you could also do a bit more to help the author think about the next draft structurally. For example, you might briefly suggest how the project could be organized around one primary question, one domain, or one analytical frame. That would make the feedback even more usable.

## Suggestions for Improvement
- Continue doing what you are already doing well: keep your focus on what the next stage of the proposal needs, not on what a finished paper would need.
- In future reviews, try adding one or two more **highly specific** observations tied to the actual wording or structure of the draft.
- Where possible, help the author see not just that something is broad or abstract, but **where** that broadness shows up most clearly.

## Overall Assessment
This is a strong peer review submission. The reviews are thoughtful, professional, constructive, and appropriately focused on the needs of developing proposals. The main thing that would make them even stronger is slightly greater specificity in a few places, but overall this is very good work.
