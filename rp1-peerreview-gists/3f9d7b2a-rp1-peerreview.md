## Overall
This is a solid submission with useful ideas in it. You clearly engaged with the proposal and identified several of the most important issues, especially around scope, validation, and what exactly the author is trying to study. There is good insight here, and your comments show that you understood the core problem the proposal was trying to address.

The main reason this score does not land higher is that the review itself is less polished and less tightly organized than the strongest submissions. The assignment asked for a structured, professional written review with distinct sections, and while your review includes many of the right ingredients, it reads a little more like an extended response than a carefully shaped review document. In other words, the feedback itself is useful, but the review would benefit from clearer structure and more controlled presentation.

## What is working well

### 1. You identify meaningful tensions in the proposal
You do a good job pointing out that the proposal still has uncertainty around validation, what exactly counts as player experience, and how that experience would actually be measured. Those are important and appropriate concerns for this stage.

### 2. You engage seriously with the topic
The review shows genuine engagement with the proposal rather than surface-level reaction. You are clearly trying to think with the project rather than dismiss it, and that makes the submission stronger.

### 3. Your feedback is generally constructive
Even where you identify weaknesses, you frame them in a way that is still helpful to the author. That matters. The review does not become hostile or dismissive.

## What to strengthen

### 1. Match the assignment structure more explicitly
The biggest issue is organizational. The assignment asked for distinct sections (summary, strengths, weaknesses/gaps, stage-appropriate feedback, suggestions, overall assessment). Your review contains versions of these ideas, but they are not always clearly separated. That makes the review feel less polished and a little harder to use.

### 2. Tighten the prose
There is some repetition and some awkward wording throughout the review. The meaning is usually recoverable, but the writing sometimes gets in the way of the feedback. Cleaner, more controlled prose would make the submission feel stronger and more professional.

### 3. Make the stage-appropriate feedback more explicit
You do give useful mid-stage guidance, but you could label and frame it more directly. For example, you might say more explicitly what the author should focus on **at this stage** versus what can wait until the final proposal. That would align the review more closely with the assignment prompt.

## Suggestions for Improvement
- In future peer reviews, follow the section structure more deliberately so the reader can immediately see each part of the response.
- Keep the helpful ideas you already have, but present them in a more controlled and concise way.
- When offering feedback, try to distinguish more clearly between:
  - what is an immediate revision priority
  - what is a later-stage concern
- Tighten wording and reduce repetition so your strongest ideas stand out more clearly.

## Overall Assessment
This review contains useful feedback and shows genuine engagement with the proposal. The main improvements needed are structural and stylistic: clearer sectioning, tighter prose, and more explicit stage-aware framing. The underlying feedback is helpful, but the review itself would be stronger if it were presented in a more polished format.
