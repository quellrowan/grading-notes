## Overall
This is a solid RP-1. The most important improvement from the earlier version is that the topic is now much better framed as a **systems/requirements problem** rather than as a topic primarily about dog training or behavior in the abstract. That reframing helps a great deal. The proposal now has a clearer center: behavior-guidance systems simplify complex and variable real-world behavior into generalized instructions, and that simplification relies on assumptions that may not hold across contexts.

That is a meaningful requirements engineering problem. It raises questions about assumptions, tradeoffs, validation, and failure conditions in systems that aim to guide behavior across diverse users and situations. The proposal is thoughtful and conceptually strong. The next step is to keep it grounded and prevent it from becoming too abstract.

This is the biggest strength of the revision. That shift makes the project feel much more aligned with requirements engineering and system design.

In particular, the idea that simplification improves usability but may obscure important variability is a strong and interesting problem statement. It creates a real design tension rather than just identifying a vague concern.

### 2. The problem statement is conceptually coherent
The proposal does a good job explaining:
- why generalized guidance is attractive
- why real-world behavior is variable
- how hidden assumptions create risk
- why this matters when users rely on systems as authoritative sources

That is a good chain of reasoning. The project reads as motivated and thoughtful, not arbitrary.

### 3. The research questions fit the problem
The questions are well aligned with the revised framing. In particular, they appropriately focus on:
- assumptions
- tradeoffs between simplicity and adaptability
- failure cases
- evaluation/validation of assumptions

That is a strong set of concerns for this topic. The questions remain centered on how systems simplify behavior and what that means for reliability.

### 4. The response to feedback is effective
You clearly explain what changed and why:
- the topic is no longer centered on dog training as the main subject

## What to strengthen

### 1. The proposal is still somewhat abstract
This is the biggest thing to improve in the next round. The project is conceptually strong, but it risks becoming too general unless you anchor it more clearly. Right now, “behavior-guidance systems” could cover a very wide range of tools:
- dog training apps
- health coaching systems
- educational guidance tools
- workplace behavior tools
- digital assistants or instructional systems

That flexibility is useful at first, but the full proposal will need a firmer center. Otherwise the project may end up discussing assumptions in a way that is interesting but too diffuse to study well.

### 2. The terms “reliability” and “effectiveness” need sharper definition
These words make sense in the problem statement, but they are still broad. In the next version, it would help to be more explicit about what counts as:
- reliable guidance
- effective guidance
- an assumption failure
- an incomplete or counterproductive result

Without that sharper definition, the project could become harder to evaluate or structure later.

### 3. Consider whether one domain should become a case context
The current version wisely avoids being “about dog training,” but the final proposal may still benefit from a concrete case context. That does not mean the topic has to become narrow in a bad way. It just means that using one domain intentionally may help:
- ground the assumptions
- identify failure scenarios
- make the evaluation discussion more concrete

The current draft is conceptually good enough to support that move in the next iteration if needed.

## Suggestions for the next iteration

### 1. Decide whether this is a broad framework project or a grounded case project
You are at the point where you will likely need to choose between:
- a broader conceptual study of assumptions in behavior-guidance systems, or
- a more grounded project using one domain as a primary case

Either approach could work, but the next version should start signaling the choice more clearly.

### 2. Make the core contribution more explicit
Right now, the project identifies an important problem, which is good. The next version should also begin to suggest what the proposal may contribute, such as:
- a way of identifying assumptions in generalized guidance systems
- a way of analyzing the tradeoff between usability and behavioral complexity
- a requirements-oriented framework for evaluating guidance reliability across contexts

You do not need a full answer yet, but the direction of contribution should become more visible.

### 3. Keep the RE lens front and center
This proposal is strongest when it stays focused on:
- assumptions
- tradeoffs
- validation
- failure cases
- context variability

Keep leaning into those. That is what makes this a software/requirements engineering project rather than a general behavioral or instructional design discussion.

## Bottom line
This is a thoughtful and improved RP-1. The reframing was absolutely the right move, and the proposal now has a much stronger systems/requirements orientation. The main task for the next iteration is to make the project a little less abstract by clarifying scope, grounding key terms, and deciding whether to anchor the study more clearly in a specific case or domain.
