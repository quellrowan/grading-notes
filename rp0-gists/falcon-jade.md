## Research Guidance Packet

### Instructor Comment
This is an interesting proposal, and one of the more distinctive ones in the set. What makes it work is that it does not just take “dog training” as a topic and stop there. It identifies a real systems problem: training apps, guides, and programs often simplify highly variable behavior into generalized instructions, and that raises questions about assumptions, validation, and failure.

That is where the proposal is strongest. The issue is not whether dogs are complicated. The issue is whether systems that communicate training guidance are built around assumptions that break down in real-world use, and what happens when users rely on those assumptions.

The main thing to watch is scope and framing. This could easily slide into a broad discussion of dog training methods if it is not kept centered on requirements questions. The strongest next version would stay focused on assumptions, system goals, and validation rather than on dog training advice in general.

### Directions to Suggest
- focus on assumptions built into one-size-fits-all training systems
- study how simplified guidance trades off usability against accuracy or adaptability
- examine what counts as validation for training systems used across very different dogs and contexts
- explore failure cases where generalized guidance leads to poor outcomes

### Scite Search Suggestions
- assumptions in behavior prediction systems
- validation of training or educational guidance systems
- personalization versus generalization in behavioral support systems
- requirements engineering assumptions validation
- one size fits all recommendation systems limitations
- human animal interaction training app evaluation

### Useful Kinds of Articles
- papers on assumptions and validation in system design
- HCI work on guidance systems, recommendation systems, or personalization
- studies of animal training apps, pet technology, or human-animal interaction systems
- papers on behavioral variability and system generalization

### Concrete Next-Step Question
Do you want to study the assumptions these systems make, or the consequences when those assumptions fail in practice? Either could work, but the project will be stronger if you decide which one is central.
