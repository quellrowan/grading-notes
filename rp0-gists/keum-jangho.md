## Research Guidance Packet

### Stronger Instructor Comment
This is a strong proposal because it begins with a concrete system, real stakeholders, and well-defined tensions rather than an abstract topic area. The strongest part of the pitch is the idea that many bypasses are not surprising edge cases but predictable misuse scenarios that may reflect requirements blind spots, assumptions, or prioritization choices. The next step is to narrow the project so it studies one slice of that problem in depth rather than trying to evaluate all of Screen Time’s failures at once.

### Promising Directions to Suggest
- Focus on misuse-case analysis for circumvention behaviors.
- Study cross-feature interactions as a source of requirements failures.
- Examine how conflicting stakeholder goals are encoded in parental control design.
- Look at assumptions about user behavior and how those assumptions fail in practice.
