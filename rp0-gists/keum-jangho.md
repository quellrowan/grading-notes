## Research Guidance Packet

### Instructor Comment
This is a strong proposal because it begins with a concrete system, real stakeholders, and a clear tension rather than just a broad topic area. The most interesting part of the pitch is the idea that many bypasses are not surprising edge cases, but predictable misuse scenarios that may reflect requirements blind spots, assumptions, or prioritization choices.

That is where the real value of this project lies. It is not just “parental controls are imperfect.” It is the more interesting question of whether these failures were foreseeable and what that says about the requirements process.

The next step is to narrow the project so it studies one slice of that problem in depth rather than trying to evaluate all of Screen Time’s weaknesses at once. If you do that, this could become a very solid project.

### Directions to Suggest
- focus on misuse-case analysis for circumvention behaviors
- study cross-feature interactions as a source of requirements failures
- examine how conflicting stakeholder goals are encoded in parental control design
- look at assumptions about user behavior and how those assumptions fail in practice
