# RP-0 Gists — Combined Review File

## Class Overview

### Overall Impression
This is a strong RP-0 set overall. Most students understood the assignment as a low-stakes exploratory pitch rather than a formal research paper, and many proposed topics that connect naturally to requirements engineering while still reflecting their personal interests.

### Strongest Patterns
- Many students selected topics with real requirements tension rather than generic software topics.
- LLM/AI topics were especially strong when framed as requirements problems rather than purely model-performance problems.
- The best proposals identified a specific gap, conflict, or curiosity and explained why requirements engineering is the right lens.
- Students generally did a decent job connecting ideas back to course concepts such as tradeoffs, misuse cases, validation, and stakeholder conflict.

### Common Weaknesses
- Some proposals remained too broad and stayed at the level of topic area rather than a researchable question.
- A few pitches explained why a domain is interesting but did less to explain the specific requirements problem inside that domain.
- Some students had clear curiosity but only a rough connection to a possible method or study direction.
- In weaker submissions, the course connection was present but fairly generic.

### What Distinguished the Strongest RP-0s
The strongest submissions did three things well:
1. Identified a specific tension, gap, or problem.
2. Explained why requirements engineering is the right way to study it.
3. Proposed a question narrow enough to imagine studying in RP-1 and beyond.

### Class-Level Coaching Note
As the course moves into the next iteration, the main opportunity is narrowing. Most students already have viable topic areas. The next step is to turn those topic areas into sharper problem statements and clearer research questions. In particular, students should move from:
- “this area is interesting”
- to “this is the specific requirements tension I want to study.”
